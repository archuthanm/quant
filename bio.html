01. The Narrative

From Code to Capital

I am a Computer Science student at King's College London with a distinct focus on the mathematical foundations of finance. While my academic roots are in algorithms and data structures, my intellectual passion lies in the stochastic nature of markets.

What draws me to quantitative finance is not prediction in the naive sense, but the discipline of making decisions under uncertainty. Markets are noisy, incomplete systems; any model is, by definition, an approximation. I am interested in how probabilistic reasoning, optimisation, and empirical testing can be used to navigate that uncertainty in a principled way - understanding not only expected returns, but the distribution of outcomes and the risks embedded within them.

My work so far reflects this mindset. Rather than treating financial models as static formulas, I approach them as objects to be stress-tested, simulated, and questioned. Whether implementing Monte Carlo methods for option pricing, replicating factor models, or learning low-level languages for performance-critical applications, my focus is on translating mathematical ideas into systems that behave predictably under real-world constraints.

Ultimately, I see quantitative finance as an applied form of scientific thinking: forming hypotheses about market behaviour, testing them against data, and refining assumptions when reality diverges from theory. I am motivated by environments where this feedback loop is taken seriously - where rigour, clarity, and intellectual honesty matter as much as technical execution.
