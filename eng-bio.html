01. The Narrative

Engineering Scalability

I am a Computer Science student at King's College London driven by the intersection of performance and design. While my foundation is in algorithms, my engineering philosophy is rooted in craftsmanship and modularity. I am dedicated to bridging the gap between the invisible machinery of distributed systems and the pixel-perfect details of the frontend - building solutions that are as resilient as they are beautiful.

My engineering philosophy centers on the trade-offs inherent in distributed systems. Software development isn't just about writing syntax; it's about making architectural decisions under constraints. I am fascinated by the friction between consistency and availability (CAP theorem) and the rigorous process required to build backends that are resilient enough to fail gracefully, yet efficient enough to process high-throughput data with minimal latency.

I approach development with a mindset of craftsmanship and automation. I believe that "it works on my machine" is a bug, not a defense. Whether I’m optimising SQL queries, containerising applications with Docker, or designing RESTful APIs, my goal is to reduce friction and eliminate fragility. I treat infrastructure as code and view maintainability as a first-class feature, ensuring that the systems I build today don't become the technical debt of tomorrow.

Ultimately, I see engineering as the art of complexity management. I am motivated by environments where high-availability isn't a buzzword but a requirement - where I can contribute to building robust, cloud-native solutions that serve users reliably without missing a heartbeat.
