1. The Objective

This project investigates one of the cornerstones of quantitative finance: the "value premium". The primary goal was to replicate the 'Value' (HML - High Minus Low) factor as defined by Fama and French. This involved sourcing data, constructing value-based portfolios, and analysing the historical performance of a long-short strategy.

2. Methodology

The HML factor was constructed using the S&P 500 as the universe. The core methodology involved annual rebalancing based on the Book-to-Market (B/M) ratio.

  • Data Sourcing: Pulled annual 'bookValue' and 'marketCap' data using `yfinance`.
  • Portfolio Sorting: Stocks were sorted by B/M ratio and split into 'High' (Value), 'Medium', and 'Low' (Growth) portfolios.
  • Factor Calculation: The HML factor is the return of the zero-investment portfolio: Return(High) - Return(Low).

3. Analysis: Factor Performance

Key performance metrics were calculated, including volatility and Sharpe ratio. Finally, a Statsmodels OLS regression was used to measure the factor's alpha and its beta exposure to the market, confirming its role as a distinct risk driver.
